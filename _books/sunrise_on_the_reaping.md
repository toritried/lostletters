The last time I read a Hunger Games book was over 10 years ago, so I hardly remember anything; however, this book was recommended to me by a friend whom I respect and who said "Collins would never write another book in the series unless she had something new to say", so I wanted to hear what that was. Boy howdy was I dissappointed. I guess the big reveal in this book is that Haymitch had a relationship with a Covey girl and had some connection to Katniss' dad. The main thesis seems to be that everything is propaganda, but did we really need a whole book to say that? Wasn't that already evident?
